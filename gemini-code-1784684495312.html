Communication Expectations

Treat these communications with a white glove and a high sense of urgency.

  • CM Updates: CM will provide updates when available.
  • Email Verification: Verify Email Address with the customer. If it is going to be delayed (SC or B&C, WO3P), communicate with the customer to pay attention to their email.
  • CSIT Escalations: If a CSIT is Urgent, after 3 hours, submit a CSIT Escalation Form to Leadership.
  • After-Hours Policy: If resolution is not met after 4 hours and is past 10 PM EST, communicate to the customer we will contact them next day.
  • Ticket Closure: If no contact is made after 2 attempts, close the ticket.
1. Opening the Call

Goal: Greet the customer, establish ownership, and verify the account status.

  • Initial Greeting: "Thank you for contacting No Service Support, my name is XXXXXX - Can I please get your name?"
  • Ownership Note: The CM who receives the call takes ownership of all communications and closes the loop. If the CM is out, the Service Partner will assist.
  • Set Expectations: Greet the customer, set expectations, and authenticate the account (If Required).
    • Authentication is required to perform the following actions, reference WCM Best Practices
      • SIM Swap
      • Request for a Port Out PIN
      • Device swap with an eSIM
      • Changing any personal identifiable information
      • Name, email address, home address, bank information, credit card information
      • Adding a Line (Customer is requesting a new line in addition to the original lines on the account)
        • Exception: If adding a line is needed to fix another issue but will be disconnected, the account does not need to be authenticated.
  • Language Barrier: If the customer requests a Spanish-speaking CM or a language barrier exists, follow the appropriate transfer protocols.
  • Documentation: If the issue needs escalation to another team (Fraud, B&C, Service Continuity, etc.) or follow-up from a Case Manager is required, including because the call was disconnected during troubleshooting, a ticket must be created.
    • Upon ticket creation, add the Transfer Accepted Tag. 
    • Add the Verified tag after verification has been completed.
2. Check for Account Issues

Goal: Determine if the problem is administrative, billing-related, or a known system-wide outage.

  • Review Interactions: Check the Insights Skittle for recent, relevant interactions.
  • Ticket History: Check Freshdesk for existing tickets for the same issue.
    • If yes, continue from where the last CM left off.
    • If none, proceed.
  • Known Issues: Check for matches in Known Issues or the !WCM Updates thread.
  • Line Status: Verify if the line is Active in all relevant tools:
    • Level 1: CApp | Level 2: Paddock | Level 3: Network (Telegence, NextGen, or Customer Portal).
      • If Not Active - Move to Suspended After Payment If the customer is suspended after paying monthly charges or after a Payment Extension request.
      • If Active in all three levels, proceed
  • App/Web Access: Move to Mobile App/Website Issues if the customer is having issues logging into the Boost Mobile app or website
  • Point of Sale Unlock: Move to Point of Sale Unlock Request if this a point of sale unlock request
  • Account Takeover: Move to Fraud Tickets if the customer is reporting an Account Takeover
  • Porting Error: Move to Porting Issues if there is a Porting error in CApp
  • International Services: Move to International Services Support if the customer is reporting issues with any international service
  • BBB/FCC/Executive Complaint: Assist using standard “No Service” procedures and work to resolve the situation.
    • If we are unable to resolve and the threat of escalation still exists, engage Leadership to escalate to Charles McKay’s Team.
3. Check Coverage

Goal: Identify if the lack of service is caused by the location, network outages, or device-network compatibility.

Tower Outages:

  • AT&T/HMN: Check MTI and AT&T Consumer site. Zoom to a 5-mile radius.
  • TMO: use Coverage -> Check Coverage on WSP (Site Performance/Maintenance/Tickets must all be Green).
    • If there is an outage, advise the customer that outages are usually resolved within 3-5 days.

Device Eligibility & Compatibility:

  • Network Compatibility: Use Customer App > Device Check to ensure the IMEI is compatible with the current network
    • If incompatible, either a Network Switch to a compatible network or a device swap to a compatible device is required.
  • International Models: Obtain the device model number via SCOUT and search using Google. 
    • Example: model # is A3410 for a Chinese iPhone 16e, Model # is A3212 for a US iPhone 16e.
    • International models may have partial compatibility with US networks & there is no guarantee that it will work. 
    • Our systems frequently show T-Mobile compatibility for these devices even though they actually have partial compatibility.
    • Do not perform a Network Switch or submit a Network Ticket
    • If troubleshooting fails, Advise a device swap to a compatible device is required.

Coverage Mapping:

  • AT&T/HMN: Use Hexbin. Green = Proceed; Blue or No Hex = Check location impact; Red = Network Switch.
    • Location Impact: Is the customer in a High Impact Location (Home/Work)? If yes, perform a Network Switch.
  • TMO: Use Coverage > Check Coverage on WSP. If Coverage is Green = Proceed, Orange/Red = Network Switch.
4. Device Troubleshooting

Goal: Verify hardware settings, SIM health, and perform physical resets.

  • Physical Inspection: Check for external/physical/liquid damage.
    • If the issue began with damage to the device, recommend replacement.
    • Remove heavy-duty or metallic cases to test reception.
  • Partial Service Issues on the T-Mobile Network
    • Advanced Tech agents will perform all necessary steps before creating a WSP ticket, but they will not create the ticket.
      • Confirm the previous agent performed troubleshooting steps. Do not have the customer complete them a second time
    • Create an Advanced Corporate Support (ACS) ticket for the issue
    • Create a WSP ticket to escalate the issue to T-Mobile
      • Open WSP and login
      • Select Create a Case > Report an Issue > Single Subscriber
      • Select the applicable issue from the Tell us more about your issue and Which error best describes your issue drop downs
      • Complete the remaining fields and submit the ticket
    • Note the WSP Ticket number (CSXXXXXXX) in your Freshdesk ticket notes
    • Provide the customer with their Freshdesk ticket number and send the IT Escalation email
    • Place the ticket into Waiting on Third Party status
  • Check for Carrier lock and TSA lock:
    • BYOD vs. RW-Boost: Use SCOUT to determine if the device is BYOD or RW-BOOST.
      • If BYOD
        • Lock Status: Use Booster for steps to check if the device is locked to another carrier.
      • If RW-Boost
        • TSA Lock: Check SCOUT (Carrier Locked Status) for Android or PIMS (Next Tether Policy) for Apple.
          • Android
            • SCOUT -> Device & SIMs -> SILS Search -> Search IMEI -> Carrier Locked Status
            • If TSA_UNLOCKED, move to TSA Locked Device
          • Apple
            • SCOUT -> PIMS Support -> Device Research -> Search IMEI -> Next Tether Policy Details
              • If US Sprint/T-Mobile Locked Policy or US Sprint/MVNO Locked Policy, move to TSA Locked Device
  • SIM Matching: Verify if the ICCID (and IMEI for eSIM) matches the Network Tool (Telegence/NextGen/Customer Portal).
    • If it does not match, activate the correct Device & SIM. Move to Device Swap issues
  • Device Settings:
    • Cellular Enabled: Ensure Airplane mode is OFF and Cellular service is ON.
    • Dual SIM Management: Ensure the correct Boost PTN/SIM is selected for Data and Voice. Disable old/unwanted PTNs.
      • Apple
        • Settings App -> Cellular
          • Make sure the correct Boost PTN is enabled (Turn on This Line toggled on)
          • Choose the default line for cellular data—tap Cellular Data, then tap the desired line
          • Choose the default line for voice calls—tap Default Voice Line, then tap desired line
      • Android
        • Settings -> Network & Internet or Connections -> SIM or Sim Manager
          • Make sure the desired SIM is enabled
          • Select the correct preferred SIM card for Calls, Text Messages, and Mobile Data (This option may not be visible if only one SIM is installed)
      • Disable any unwanted PTNs/SIMs no longer in use from a previous carrier
  • Signal Indicators: Check bars (has bars vs. SOS/Emergency Calls Only/No SIM)
  • The "Quick Fixes":
    • Toggle Airplane Mode: On for 15 seconds, then Off.
      • Turning Airplane mode on will Power down the RF and Transceiver in the device and terminate the active session with both the network core & tower. Once turned off, the phone will re-attach to the strongest available tower and reinitiate the handshake procedure with the network core. 
        • After this step is performed, the tower will treat the device with a higher than normal priority for network traffic in order to establish the connection. It will move the device back to the standard priority level after three minutes.
      • If this step appears to resolve the issue, wait three minutes to ensure the resolution is not a temporary fix. 
    • Power Cycle: Full Power Off for 30 seconds, then Power On.
      • Perform this step ONLY if the device feels sluggish, unresponsive, the device is hot to the touch, or the battery drains quickly. These symptoms are indicative of a potential device issue.
    • If these steps are a temporary fix, proceed to the next step.
  • Android Only: Install the Boost Config App
    • If the device is Android and BYOD, confirm that the Boost Config App has been downloaded and installed on the device.
      • Yes - Use Ozmo to ensure that the Boost APN is selected in the device’s setting
      • No - Install the Boost Config App
        • Connect to Wi-Fi > Download Boost Config App > Install > Restart Device
    • Note: If the customer is unable to connect to WiFi, the APNs must be manually entered. Ask Booster how to enter the appropriate network’s APNs on the device.
  • Apple Only: Update Carrier bundle & Software
    • Ensure that the software is iOS 18.4 or above and that there are no carrier updates available. 
      • You will have to connect to a WiFi network for the update. 
      • The customer may need to manage their device’s storage to have enough room to download the update.
      • Update Software: Settings > General > Software Update> Update Now
      • Carrier Settings: Go to Settings > General > About > wait 30 seconds for the pop-up.
      • Beta software is not supported.
        • Go to General > Software Update > Beta Updates > Off
  • Technical Symptom redirection: 
    • Troubleshoot any remaining issues in this order: 
      • No Sim Detected
      • SIM not supported
      • SOS/Emergency Calls Only
      • Voice
      • Data
      • Messaging
5. After service is restored
  1. If the Issue was resolved on the call and a ticket was created
    1. Click the checkbox “Resolved by Live CM”
    2. Continue
  2. Only had Service issue
    1. Add note on the account and ticket work notes.
    2. Resolve ticket following normal steps.
    3. Do not change the Does the Customer Currently Have Service? field.
  3. If the service issue is resolved but needs an account clean up.
    1. Add Note on the account.
    2. Change Status of ticket to appropriate status (waiting on third party, tied to inc, etc.).
    3. Change Support Status of ticket to Reconciliation (will not impact SLA Alarms).
    4. Transfer the ticket to the WCM 2/3 group.
    5. When the ticket is ready to be resolved, update the Status (not Support Status).
    6. Do not change the Does the Customer Currently Have Service? field.
